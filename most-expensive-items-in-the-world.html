2. The Little Mermaid Statue

The Little Mermaid statue was created by Danish sculptor Edvard Eriksen, and it was first unveiled at the Copenhagen World’s Fair in 1913. The statue is made of copper and weighs about 250 kg (about 550 lbs).

The original Little Mermaid statue is located in Copenhagen’s harbor. It depicts a young mermaid sitting on a rock, holding her hands together in front of her chest as she stares into an underwater world.

In 1989, the sculpture was stolen from its pedestal; but it was recovered six months later after police received an anonymous phone call about its whereabouts. An investigation revealed that the thief had sold it for $250 USD dollars to someone who wanted to restore it! The buyer then sold it again—this time for one million dollars USD!

3. Faberge Egg

The Faberge Egg is a jewelled Easter egg made under the supervision of the Russian jeweller Peter Carl Faberge. It contains 5,900 diamonds and 27 sapphires and is valued at $100 million. It was first made in 1885 for Alexander III as an Easter gift for his wife Maria Feodorovna. The egg has been owned by many people but it was sold at an auction to Viktor Vekselberg in 2004 for $50 million.

4. Renoir’s Bal au moulin de la Galette

Renoir’s Bal au moulin de la Galette is one of the most famous paintings in history and is valued at $100 million.

In this painting, Renoir depicts a scene during the last day of summer in 1876, when the Moulin de la Galette (a dance hall located at Butte Montmartre) was still open to everyone regardless of their social status. The painter paints with an elegant touch and depicts ordinary people in an extraordinary way, using subtle colors and shades to create a mesmerizing work of art that will leave you breathless!
