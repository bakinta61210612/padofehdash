Top Facts about Nannie Doss

1. Nannie Doss (born Nancy Hazel; November 4, 1905 – June 2, 1965) was an American serial killer responsible for the deaths of 11 people.
2. Nannie was born in Blue Mountain, Alabama, now part of Anniston.
3. She was one of five children with one brother and three sisters.
4. At age 9, she suffered severe headaches, blackouts and depression after hitting her head on a metal bar on a train.
5. She married her first husband Charley Braggs at age 16, producing four daughters in total.
6. Braggs and Nannie’s two middle daughters died due to suspected food poisoning.
7. Nannie married Robert Harrelson in 1929, who was an alcoholic and had a criminal record for assault.
8. Melvina gave birth to Robert Lee Haynes in 1943 and Nannie took out a life insurance policy on him.
9. Robert Lee Haynes died mysteriously under Nannie’s care on July 7, 1945.
10. Nannie married Arlie Lanning in 1945 and had their house burn down, receiving insurance money for it.
11. Nannie’s third husband, Lanning, died of what was said to be heart failure.
12. Nannie met her fourth husband, Morton, through a lonely-heart column in 1952. She poisoned him with rat poison in 1953.
13. Nannie married her fifth husband, Samuel Doss of Tulsa, Oklahoma, in June 1953.
14. Samuel Doss died suddenly in October 1954 due to arsenic in his system.
15. Nannie Doss pleaded guilty to murder on May 17, 1955 and was sentenced to life imprisonment; she died in prison in 1965.

In all, authorities suspect she killed as many as 12 people, most of whom were blood-related. Doss blamed her murderous escapades on her brain injury.
